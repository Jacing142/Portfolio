GUARDRAILS

Production systems fail in boring ways

Someone uploads the wrong file. An API times out. An edge case appears on day one. I design for those paths as carefully as the happy one.

The goal isn't to take people out of the process. It's to take out the repetitive work while keeping the decisions that matter visible and reviewable.

When the model is confident and wrong

Outputs are scored against defined checks before release. Below a threshold, nothing ships. It goes to a person with the full context attached, not a bare flag saying something needs checking.

When the output isn't good enough

Generated work has to meet defined quality criteria before release. Anything that falls short goes to review rather than out the door.

When the input is wrong

Validation at intake catches bad or ambiguous input before it enters the workflow.

When something breaks

Retries and fallbacks keep work moving where possible. Silent failure is the worst outcome, so failed runs are surfaced rather than swallowed.

When the data is sensitive

Sensitive information is filtered before it reaches a model. Access follows least-privilege principles, and your data stays in your systems.

When someone needs to override it

Nothing runs that your team cannot stop, change or switch off. Decisions are logged and reviewable.

When someone has to explain a decision

An automated decision your staff cannot explain is a decision they cannot stand behind. Escalations carry the reasoning that triggered them, and outputs trace back to the criteria that produced them.

EVALUATION AND MONITORING

How I know it works

Systems are tested against real cases in a sandbox and must clear agreed quality thresholds before they go near production. A system nobody has measured isn't a system, it's a demo.

What it did, why, and what it cost

Every run is logged: what came in, what the system did with it, where it stopped. When something goes wrong you can see which step failed rather than reconstructing it from the output. Cost is tracked per run, so spend is a number you can see rather than a surprise at the end of the month.
